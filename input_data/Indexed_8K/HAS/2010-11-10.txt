EXHIBIT 99
At its 2010 Investor Day, (NYSE: HAS) outlined its blueprint to becoming a branded play company.  The meeting included presentations from Brian Goldner, Hasbro's President & CEO, Deb Thomas, CFO, David Hargreaves, COO and other members of Hasbro's senior management team.  
Pawtucket, RI (November 9, 2010) 
Hasbro,
Inc.
"Hasbro continues to execute its long-term strategy towards becoming a branded play company," said Brian Goldner, Hasbro's President and CEO.  "By re-imagining, re-inventing and re-igniting our core brands as well as imagining new brands, Hasbro is activating its brands globally through innovative toys, games and immersive experiences as well as the expansion of these brands into television and motion picture entertainment, digital gaming and lifestyle licensing.  Today's meeting was an opportunity to share with investors our plans for the near and long-term."  
During today's meeting, the company reaffirmed its belief that it should be able to grow revenues and earnings per share for the full year 2010, including the dilution from its television investments and barring a further decline in consumer spending, global economic conditions or foreign exchange.
Additionally, the company established guidance for 2011, commenting that it believes it should be able to grow revenues and earnings per share in 2011 barring a decline in global economic conditions, consumer spending or foreign exchange.
Hasbro's Investor Day was held at its Pawtucket, RI headquarters.  The meeting was webcast live on the company's website at http://investor.hasbro.com, where a replay of the event is also available.
