Other Events. On July 6, OrthoNeutrogena, a division of Ortho-McNeil Pharmaceutical, Inc., a Johnson & Johnson company, acquired Colbar LifeScience Ltd., a privately held biotechnology company specializing in reconstructive medicine and tissue engineering. Colbar, which is based in Herzliya, Israel, currently markets two products that use its proprietary GlymatrixTM technology: EvolenceTM, a collagen for facial wrinkles that is sold in Europe, Canada and Japan and OssixTM, a dental membrane for bone regeneration that is sold in the US and Europe. As a result of the acquisition Johnson & Johnson expects to incur an estimated one-time after-tax charge of approximately $50 million, or $.02 per share, related to the expensing of in-process research and development. Other terms of the transaction were not disclosed.
