EXHIBIT 99.1 Contact: Neil Lefort Vice President, Investor Relations Tel: (630) 527-4344 MOLEX DIRECTORS RENEW STOCK REPURCHASE AUTHORIZATION Lisle, IL, USA - July 28, 2003 - Molex Incorporated (NASDAQ: MOLX and MOLXA), a global electronic components company, today announced that its Board of Directors has renewed its authorization to permit certain discretionary stock purchases by the Company. The authorization allows the purchase of the Company's outstanding Common Stock (MOLX) and/or Class A Common Stock (MOLXA) during the fiscal year ending June 30, 2004 up to an aggregate value of $100 million. During the year ended June 30, 2003, under a similar $100 million authorization, the Company purchased 3.4 million shares of the Company's (MOLXA) outstanding stock with a total purchase value of $75.0 million. According to Fred A. Krehbiel, Co-Chairman, "The number of shares of stock purchased during fiscal 2004, will depend upon various factors including the relative price of the stock and future uses of cash including acquisitions." Molex Incorporated is a 65-year-old manufacturer of electronic, electrical and fiber optic interconnection products and systems; switches; value-added assemblies; and application tooling. Based in Lisle, Illinois, USA, the Company operates 55 manufacturing facilities in 19 countries and employs approximately 17,275 people. Editor's note: Molex is traded on the NASDAQ National Market System in the United States, on the London Stock Exchange and (MOLX) is included in the S&P 500 Index and the NASDAQ 100.
